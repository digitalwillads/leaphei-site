Knowledge center · Glossary

The vocabulary of a home equity investment.

A plain-English definition for every term you'll see in a Leap contract, application, or comparison piece. Sourced directly from Leap's published glossary.

Adjustable Rate Mortgage
A mortgage in which the interest rate changes with the market without the need to refinance.
Amortization
A calculated series of monthly payments in order to pay off your fixed-rate or adjustable rate mortgage.
Annual Percentage Rate (APR)
An interest rate that reflects the yearly cost of a mortgage. This rate may be higher than the initial or advertised rate because it takes discount points and other credit costs into account.
Appraisal
An estimated value of a property made by a qualified appraiser that incorporates market value, road maintenance, sewage and other infrastructure factors.
Cash-Out Equity
When a financial institution pays the remaining balance of your mortgage in exchange for equity in your home.
Debt-to-Income Ratio
A ratio, expressed as a percentage, of a borrower's monthly payment obligations for long-term debts divided by the borrower's effective net income (for FHA/VA loans) or gross monthly income (for conventional loans).
Deferred Interest
When a homeowner's monthly mortgage payments do not satisfy the mortgage note rate, this unpaid interest is then deferred by adding it to the loan balance.
Discount Points
Points are fees paid to a lender in exchange for a reduced interest rate. A single point is typically sold for 1% of your total loan amount.
Fannie Mae
Federal National Mortgage Association
Ginnie Mae
Government National Mortgage Association
Freddie Mac
Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corporation
FICO Score
A form of credit score which takes data from Equifax, Exeprian, and Transunion.
Fixed Rate Mortgage
A mortgage in which the interest rate is fixed at the time of the loan, this interest rate can be changed by refinancing your mortgage.
Hard Inquiry
When applying for a new line of credit, lenders will check your credit history to determine any risk.
Home-Equity Line of Credit (HELOC)
When the owner of a piece of property borrows money against the equity of that property on an as needed basis.
Impound
A portion of a borrower's monthly payments held by the bank to pay for taxes, hazard/mortgage insurance, lease payments or any other outstanding fees.
Lien
A penalty that is placed on a piece of property by a bank, lender, contractor or government entity until the payment or satisfaction of a debt or financial obligation to that entity.
Loan-to-Value Ratio (LTV)
The relationship between the amount of a mortgage loan and the appraised value of the property expressed in a percentage.
Mortgage Insurance
Money paid monthly by the homeowner to insure a mortgage.
Negative Amortization
Occurs when a homeowners' monthly payments are not large enough to pay the interest due on the loan.
Reverse Mortgage
Typically reserved for homeowners aged 60+, reverse mortgages allow homeowners to borrow money against the equity of their property.
Second Mortgage
A loan that utilizes the property as collateral and incurs a mortgage lien on the property.
Secure Line of Credit
A line of credit that is attached to a piece of property.
Short Sale
When a homeowner sells their property for less than the value of their mortgage.
Unsecured Line of Credit
A line of credit that is not attached to a piece of property.
Utilization Rate
How much you owe on your credit vs your credit limit.
