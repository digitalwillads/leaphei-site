Leap Revive · The Renewal

Renovate the home you love. Without the renovation loan.

For homeowners who want to update kitchens, bathrooms, or whole-home systems without adding a monthly payment to a budget that already runs the household. Leap Revive funds the project in full at close and leaves your monthly outflow untouched.

Who Leap Revive is for

You love your home and you plan to stay. But the kitchen is dated, or the primary bath is past its useful life, or the HVAC is twenty-two years old and limping. You have the equity to fund a renovation — but the construction loans you've looked at would add $1,400 to $2,200 in monthly payments for the next fifteen years, which is enough to delay the project indefinitely.

Revive removes that obstacle. The renovation happens. The monthly budget stays where it is.

How Revive works

Leap invests $50,000 to $300,000 in your home's equity. Funds are released at closing — no draw schedule, no waiting on contractor invoices, no construction-loan complexity. You manage the renovation. We do not.

Because Revive funds are released in full, you have negotiating leverage with contractors and material suppliers that draw-based financing cannot match. Many of our Revive customers report saving 8–15% on the project cost simply by paying suppliers up front.

The term

Revive is a 15-year investment. The longer term reflects the typical hold period for renovated homes — most homeowners who renovate plan to live in the result for at least a decade. You can repay early at any time with no penalty.

Worked example

A homeowner in Pittsburgh, PA.

Diane and Tom own a 1962 split-level worth $385,000 with $260,000 of equity. The kitchen is original. They have a contractor quote of $128,000 for a full kitchen renovation, refinished hardwood throughout the main floor, and HVAC replacement.

HELOC alternative

Loan amount $128,000
Interest rate 9.1%
Monthly payment (15yr) $1,304
Total interest paid $106,720

Leap Revive

Investment $140,000
Cash to renovation $128,000
Monthly payment $0
Interest accrued $0

Settled in year 12 when Diane and Tom downsized. Home sold for $520,000, $135,000 appreciation. Repayment to Leap: $140,000 + 30% of $135,000 = $180,500. The HELOC would have cost them $234,720 in payments over 15 years.
