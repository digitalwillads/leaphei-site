Chapter 01 · The Mechanics

A home equity investment, fully explained.

Most homeowners have only encountered HELOCs and cash-out refinancing. A home equity investment is structurally different. This page is the long version — written so you can decide with full information.

Day 0
1
Step One

Apply and qualify

A three-question pre-qualification confirms eligibility in under two minutes, and a full application then runs an automated credit and equity check without the income statements, employment verification, or paperwork backlog that a traditional mortgage process tends to require.

Within days
2
Step Two

Receive your investment

Once approved, the investment funds at closing, typically within days, and up to $300,000 in cash is deposited directly to your account for any purpose you choose.

Years 1–30
3
Step Three

Live without a new payment

For the full term of the investment, anywhere from ten to thirty years depending on the product, you make no monthly payments and accrue no interest, so the investment never shows up on your household budget the way a HELOC or refinance would.

At sale
4
Step Four

Repay when you choose

When you sell the home, refinance, or reach the end of the term, you repay the original investment plus a share of your home's appreciation since the investment began.

The math

How the investment is calculated

The amount Leap can invest depends on two figures: your home's appraised value and the equity you already hold in it. We invest in the equity, not the underlying mortgage.

For most homeowners, Leap invests between 10 and 25 percent of the current appraised value, up to a $300,000 maximum. The exact figure depends on the product, your credit profile, the local market, and the term length you choose.

The repayment formula

One investment, walked through end to end

The formula has two parts: your original investment back, plus an agreed share of the appreciation. If your home does not appreciate, you owe only the original amount.

If values fall

Leap shares the downside, not just the upside

If your home appraises lower at the end of the investment than it did at the start, your repayment is reduced. In most products on a one-for-one basis. In the steepest declines, repayment can fall below the original investment.

This is the structural advantage of an investment over a loan. A loan must be repaid in full regardless of the home's market value. An investment shares the risk because it shares the upside.

Terms and flexibility

What you can do, what we ask

Early repayment

Repay any time, no penalty

You can settle a Leap investment at any point during the term. The amount due uses the same formula: the original investment plus the agreed share of appreciation as of the repayment date, based on a current appraisal.

Your obligations

Live in the home. Stay current.

For the term of the investment, you occupy the home as your primary residence, keep it in reasonable condition, and stay current on your primary mortgage, property taxes, and homeowners insurance. There are no other ongoing obligations.
