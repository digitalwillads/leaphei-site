Our Mission · Equity, Where It Belongs

Equity belongs to the homeowners who built it.

Our mission is to close the wealth gap by empowering historically underserved homeowners. The wealth stored in American homes belongs to the families who built it, brick by brick, payment by payment, decade by decade.

$35T in home equity sits in American homes — the single largest store of household wealth in the country.

The wealth that sits in homes

For most American families, the equity in their home is worth more than their retirement accounts, stock portfolios, and savings combined.

But for the homeowners who hold it, that equity has historically been one of the hardest assets to access. Traditional products require strong current income, accept new monthly payments onto the household budget, or transfer ownership of the home to a lender.

For homeowners on fixed incomes, between jobs, or simply unwilling to take on new debt, those products put their own equity out of reach.

What we believe
“Community, not exploitation, is the nature of capitalism in the 21st century: inclusive capitalism.”
The principle Leap is built on.
History

Equity, redlining, and the missing trillion

The history of American home equity is not equally distributed. Federal redlining policies, enforced from the 1930s through the 1960s, systematically denied mortgage credit to Black and Latino families in specific neighborhoods.

The effect compounded across generations. Families denied access to homeownership in those decades had no equity to pass down. Their children entered adulthood without the down-payment support that built wealth for their neighbors. Their grandchildren start with the same disadvantage today.

Leap is not a fix for redlining. No product is. But we are explicitly built to serve homeowners in markets and demographics that traditional equity lenders have historically underserved, with underwriting that prioritizes equity ownership over income documentation, and a product structure that does not add a monthly payment to a budget that may already be stretched.

~$1T

The missing trillion dollars of equity that, absent redlining, would have accumulated in the homes of Black and Latino American families.

Federal Reserve · Urban Institute

Black homeowners hold roughly half the equity of white homeowners — even after controlling for income and home value.

What we won’t do

No predatory targeting. Ever.

Leap does not buy lead lists of distressed homeowners. We do not run direct-response advertising to households in foreclosure. We do not offer “investment” products that are reverse mortgages by another name — with compounding interest, lien-style claims, or balance-erodes-equity mechanics.

Every Leap product is a true equity investment: downside shared, no compounding, no monthly obligation. Every contract is written in plain English. Every appraisal is conducted by a third-party licensed appraiser. Every origination is reviewed against fair-lending standards stricter than what the law requires.

What we’re working toward

Knowledge is Power.

Leap unlocks the equity in your home to help you achieve financial wellness. We combine advanced financial technology with institutional real estate expertise to improve financial literacy, remove bias from the application process through AI and ML, and put cash to work paying down debt instead of adding to it.

A home equity account that is as accessible to the family who built it as a checking account — no strings, no monthly payments, no surrendering the home itself. That is the work. That is what every Leap investment is engineered to advance.

We believe in teamwork — and having fun when we’re not grinding on your behalf.
